Clustering in bipartite graphs

Clustering Coefficient

  • Watts/Strogatz 1998: Collective dynamics of ‘small-world’ networks
  • Measure of cohesion
  • Defines "cliquishness of a typical neighbourhood" (Watts/Strogatz 1998, 440)
  • High Clustering Coefficient associated with
    • dense (social) structures
    • efficiency, effectiveness
    • tendency towards homogeneity
    • Beeinflussungs- und Konsensbildungsprozesse, Grundlage für These Informationsredundanz von Gruppenakteuren sowie Annahme Fähigkeit zu kollektiver Aktion

Bimodal Clustering Coefficient

6-cycles and 4-paths?

  • 6-cycles for M1-1: 8
  • 4-paths for M1-1: 6
  • → Clustering Coefficient: range not necessarily 0 - 1
  • 5-paths for M1-1: 8

Bimodal Clustering Coefficient

6-cycles and 5-paths

  • Graph-theoretical: more branches in 6-cycles than in 4-paths, if network strongly connected; 5-paths same amount of branches as 6-cycles → coefficient between 0 and 1
  • Network-theoretical: 5-paths give possible relationships between 3 nodes, not just general possibility of relationship
  • Opsahl not necessarily wrong, just different interpretation, coefficient not between 0 and 1
  • equivalent to squares/possible squares
  • Clustering coefficient concept native to bimodal case

Bimodal Clustering Coefficient

6-cycles and stars

Preliminary results use case:

  • Projection Objects: 30,90% of triads from 6-cycles, 69,10% from stars
  • Projection Graves: 74,53% of triads from 6-cycles, 25,47% from stars
